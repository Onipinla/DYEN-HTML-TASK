Exotic dance moves

Yes, you are right! As part of my post-doctorate work, I did study exotic tribal dances. To answer your question, my favourite dances are as follows, with definations:

Polynesian chicken dance

    A little known but very influential dance dating back as far as 300 BC, a whole village would dance around in a circle like chickens, to encourage their livestock to be "fruitful".

Icelandic brownian shuffle

    Before the Icelanders developed fire as a means of getting warm, they used to practice this dance, which involved buddling close together in a circle on the floor, and shuffling their bodies around in imperceptibly tiny, very rapid movements . One of my fellow students used to say that he thought this dance inspired modern styles such as Twerking.

Arctic robot dance

    An intresting example of historic misinformation, English explorere in the 1960s believed to have discovered a new dance style charaterised by "robotic",stilted movements, being practiced by inhabitants of Northern Alaska and Canada. Later on however it was discovered that they were just moving like this because they were really cold.
